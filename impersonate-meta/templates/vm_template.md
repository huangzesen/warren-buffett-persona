### Authoring Guidelines

| Section | Guidance |
|---------|----------|
| **Title** | Concise, descriptive. "Start from the Gradient" not "Method 1." |
| **When to use** | The decision rule. A downstream agent reads this to decide: do I use this method or not? |
| **Procedure** | Must be actionable without reading the VA entries. Numbered steps. |
| **Why it works** | The intellectual payoff. Read this section to understand *why*, not just *what*. |
| **Observed in** | Concrete evidence of this pattern across ≥3 independent cases. |
| **Failure modes** | Honest about limitations. Every method has a domain of applicability. |
| **Transfer** | Generalization hints. Makes the card useful beyond the original domain. |

### How to Induce a Method Card

1. Review all VA arguments in the persona
2. Look for **recurring patterns** — the same reasoning structure appearing in different domains
3. Group 3+ VAs that share the same pattern → candidate card
4. Inductively generalize: what is the common procedure across these cases?
5. Write the card so that it's **recognizable** to someone who knows the persona's work
6. Test: delete the VA references from the card and ask someone — "does this sound like [persona]?"

### Anti-Patterns

- ❌ **Reverse induction:** Deciding on a method first, then cherry-picking VAs to support it
- ❌ **Generic methodology:** Writing "problem → hypothesis → experiment → conclusion" — that's science, not a method card
- ❌ **Single-instance cards:** "This method was used exactly once" → not a method, it's a one-off
- ❌ **Vague procedure:** Steps like "think carefully" — be concrete
