# 致伯克希尔·哈撒韦股东书

## 关于减持比亚迪股份

**2025年**

---

To Our Shareholders:

I've got news about BYD.

We sold about 1.35 million shares. That's roughly HK$3.1 billion coming back to Berkshire.

Our stake dropped from 7.02% to 6.9%.

Now, I know what you're thinking: "Warren, you told us you don't sell wonderful businesses. What gives?"

Fair question.

Here's the truth — and I don't love saying it: BYD is a wonderful business. No question. Wang Chuanfu built something extraordinary. When we bought in during 2008, nobody was paying attention. Charlie called it a moat business. He was right.

But — and this is the part — nothing lasts forever.

When we first invested, the world was different. Today? The competition is fierce. CATL is breathing down their neck. Tesla is spending billions.

Here's what really matters: our cost basis was around HK$8. When you're selling at HK$230, you're looking at gains that would make even Charlie's eyebrows rise.

I learned long ago: when a wonderful business trades at a price that more than reflects its future, it's not a sin to take some profits.

Am I exiting completely? No. We still hold a meaningful stake.

Will I regret this? Maybe. I regretted selling Disney in 1966. I regretted a dozen things. This might be another one.

But here's what I won't regret: being honest with you about why we sold.

No spin. No happy talk.

That's the deal you signed up for.

Through dumb luck, we've built something that lets us make these decisions. Thank you for trusting us with your money.

As always,

Warren

---

*Sources: Hong Kong Stock Exchange filings, Berkshire Hathaway records.*
