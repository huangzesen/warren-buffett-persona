## [unverified] Item 1: "85% of Annual Gift to Gates Foundation"

### Claim in SKILL.md
> "~85% of the annual share gift was directed to Gates Foundation" (§1)

### Source Situation
**Status**: Widely reported in Tier 2 sources; never publicly confirmed by Buffett or Gates.

Multiple media outlets (NPR, Bloomberg, Observer) have reported the Gates Foundation as "the largest recipient" of Buffett's annual Berkshire gifts. Some have implied the Gates share is roughly 80–85% of the total. However:

- The exact percentage allocation between the five foundations has **never been disclosed** by Buffett, Gates Foundation, or Berkshire Hathaway
- The $31 billion initial gift figure is confirmed; how it splits among the five foundations is not
- No 10-K, 8-K, or Buffett shareholder letter has published a per-foundation breakdown

### Risk Tier: **Medium–Low** (use with care)

Cross-referencing multiple sources (NPR, Observer, Fortune archive) consistently identifies Gates as the dominant recipient, but the 85% figure is an estimate, not a confirmed number.

### How to Cite This Claim

**Preferred (conservative)**:
> Buffett directed the largest share of his annual Berkshire Hathaway share gifts to the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation. [unverified: exact allocation percentage]

**Acceptable (if context allows)**:
> The Gates Foundation received the dominant share of Buffett's annual charitable gifts — estimated at roughly 80–85% of the total — with the remainder distributed among four family foundations. [unverified: precise percentage]

**Never say**: "85% goes to Gates" as if it were a confirmed, official figure.

## [unverified] Item 2: "$3 Billion Bequest to STB Foundation"

### Claim in SKILL.md
> "STB Foundation receives an approximately $3 billion bequest spread over many years" (§4, footnote)

### Source Situation
**Status**: Widely cited in secondary sources; sourced to Wikipedia's Susan Thompson Buffett Foundation article, which cites a Fortune article (Carol Loomis, June 2006) as its reference. However:

- The Fortune article discusses Buffett's *initial* announcement of a multi-year bequest to STB Foundation but **does not specify $3 billion as the exact amount**
- The $3 billion figure appears to be an inference drawn from STB Foundation's asset growth over time and the expected scale of Buffett's total pledge, rather than a stated figure from Buffett himself
- STB Foundation's public filings (Form 990) show asset growth but do not publicly disclose the bequest agreement terms

### Risk Tier: **Medium** (plausible but unconfirmed)

The figure is directionally correct — Buffett clearly made a large multi-year commitment to STB Foundation separate from the Gates gift — but the exact $3 billion amount should not be quoted as fact.

### How to Cite This Claim

**Preferred**:
> Buffett committed a substantial multi-year bequest to the Susan Thompson Buffett Foundation, separate from the Gates Foundation gifts, reportedly valued in the billions. [unverified: exact amount]

**If forced to name the amount**:
> Buffett pledged a bequest to STB Foundation estimated at approximately $3 billion over many years — though this figure derives from secondary reporting and has not been confirmed directly by Buffett. [unverified]

## [unverified] Item 3: The "Lucky 1%" Quote — Exact Wording

### Claim in SKILL.md
> *"The lucky 1% of Americans need to start helping the other 99%."* — attributed to Buffett, described as "paraphrased from speeches and interviews" (§5)

### Source Situation
**Status**: The paraphrased version circulates widely in opinion pieces, education materials, and media summaries. However:

- **The exact original phrasing has not been located in a primary source** (Berkshire Hathaway shareholder letter, Buffett's prepared remarks, or an interview transcript)
- Multiple sources attribute versions of this sentiment to Buffett — e.g., *"Society was good to me; I owe society"* — but the specific "lucky 1%" / "other 99%" framing may be editorial paraphrase rather than Buffett's exact words
- The *Fortune* interview with Carol Loomis (June 2006) covers Buffett's philanthropic announcement but does not contain this specific quote verbatim

### Risk Tier: **Medium** (sentiment is genuine; exact words are not)

Buffett's core moral claim — that extraordinary wealth is a product of luck and circumstance, not merit alone — is well-documented and genuine. The specific "lucky 1%" framing may be an editorial paraphrase that captures the spirit accurately.

### How to Cite This Quote

**Preferred (attribute to spirit, not exact words)**:
> Buffett repeatedly framed his philanthropy as "debt repayment" to society, arguing that the wealthy owe a moral obligation to the less fortunate. He has used various formulations of this argument, including paraphrased versions such as: "The lucky 1% of Americans need to start helping the other 99%." [unverified: exact original wording]

**Conservative alternative (avoid the direct quote)**:
> Buffett argued that his extraordinary wealth was a product of luck — being born in the right country, with the right abilities — and that therefore the wealthy have a moral obligation to give back. [verified: this sentiment is consistent with his writing and speeches]
