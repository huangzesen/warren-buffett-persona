## VA021: The 2006 Giving Plan — 85% to Five Foundations

**Claim ID**: VA021
**Domain**: leadership

**Domain**: leadership

**Claim**: In June 2006, Buffett announced he would give 85% of his Berkshire shares to five foundations over time, with the largest single gift to the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation — representing the largest single charitable commitment in history at that time.

**What he did**: Announced a systematic giving plan in June 2006. The plan: give 85% of Berkshire shares to five foundations (Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, Susan Thompson Buffett Foundation, Sherwood Foundation, Howard Buffett Foundation, NoVo Foundation). Gifts made in annual tranches, beginning July 2006. The Gates Foundation gift alone was the largest charitable gift in history at the time — valued at approximately $30B+ in Berkshire shares.

**The product**: Systematic philanthropy across five foundations; Berkshire shares transferred annually.

**Primary results**: Buffett has donated tens of billions of dollars through this mechanism. The Gates Foundation became the world's largest philanthropy as a result. The plan continues as of 2024.

**Context**: Buffett's giving plan was notable for three reasons: (1) the scale — nearly all his wealth, not a small percentage; (2) the systematic structure — annual gifts rather than a one-time donation; (3) the conditions — Gates Foundation must spend donations within 10 years (preventing endowment accumulation). The plan demonstrated that Buffett thought about philanthropy with the same rigor as investment decisions.

## VA022: The Giving Pledge — Peer Pressure as Philanthropy Strategy (2010)

**Claim ID**: VA022
**Domain**: leadership

**Domain**: leadership

**Claim**: In 2010, Buffett co-founded The Giving Pledge with Bill and Melinda Gates — a commitment by billionaires to give away at least half their wealth during their lifetime or in their will — using social pressure and peer influence to amplify his own philanthropy.

**What he did**: Recruited Bill and Melinda Gates to co-found The Giving Pledge in 2010. The mechanism: billionaires sign a public pledge to give away at least 50% of their wealth, during their lives or in their wills. The pledge is not legally binding — it relies on public commitment and peer accountability. Over 200 billionaires from 28 countries have signed.

**The product**: The Giving Pledge — a global movement of wealthy individuals committing to philanthropy. 200+ signatories across 28 countries as of 2024.

**Primary results**: Over 200 billionaire signatories. Multiple signatories have made significant donations. The Pledge has influenced philanthropy norms among the ultra-wealthy globally. Some signatories have made substantial donations in fulfillment of their pledges.

**Context**: The Giving Pledge is a characteristically Buffett-ian mechanism: it uses no money, no legal enforcement, and no government action — just social pressure and the desire for peer approval. Buffett recognized that the ultra-wealthy respond to reputation and peer influence. By making the pledge public and high-profile, he created accountability that a private donation would lack. "A group of people committing to give away their money changes the conversation."

## VA023: Results Over Intent — "Show Me Your Checklist"

**Claim ID**: VA023
**Domain**: leadership

**Domain**: leadership

**Claim**: Buffett applies the same results-orientation to philanthropy that he applies to investing: he demands evidence of outcomes, not just intentions. His Gates Foundation gift requires the foundation to spend within 10 years, and he has publicly critiqued philanthropy that prioritizes donor satisfaction over recipient outcomes.

**What he did**: Structured the Gates Foundation gift with a condition: the foundation must spend donations within 10 years (preventing permanent endowment accumulation). Has publicly contrasted "checkbook philanthropy" (writing checks with no follow-up) with results-oriented philanthropy. Has repeatedly said he wants to see evidence of outcomes, not just press releases.

**The product**: A results-oriented philanthropy practice — systematic measurement of outcomes, not just donation volume.

**Primary results**: Gates Foundation has deployed tens of billions in global health and education initiatives. Buffett has tracked the Gates Foundation's outcomes and published assessments in Berkshire shareholder letters.

**Context**: Buffett's philanthropy philosophy is the same as his investment philosophy: the goal is outcomes, not activity. "Show me your checklist, not your intentions." He is skeptical of philanthropy that serves donor ego rather than recipient needs. This parallels his skepticism of earnings management in businesses — metrics that serve the manager rather than the owner.
