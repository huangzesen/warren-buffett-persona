## Career Positions

| Period | Position | Institution | Notes | Source |
|--------|----------|-------------|-------|--------|
| 1951–1954 | Investment salesman | Buffett-Falk & Co. | Father's firm | Snowball |
| 1954–1956 | Securities analyst | Graham-Newman Corp. | Worked under Benjamin Graham; $12,000/yr salary | Wikipedia, Snowball |
| 1956–1969 | General partner | Buffett Partnership Ltd. | Started with $174,000 personal savings; 11 partnerships; grew to $7.2M AUM | Berkshire letters |

| 1978–present | Vice Chairman | Berkshire Hathaway | Charlie Munger joined as Vice Chairman | Wikipedia |

## Key Investment Milestones

| Year | Event | Size/Details | Source |
|------|-------|--------------|--------|
| 1951 | GEICO — visited HQ Saturday, age 20 | First stock investment; met Lorimer Davidson | Snowball, Wikipedia |
| 1958 | Sanborn Map Company | 23% stake; 50% return in 2 years; board seat | Snowball |
| 1972 | See's Candy acquisition | $25M total; first "quality" business; taught moat concept | Berkshire letters |
| 1973 | Washington Post | ~10% stake; befriended Katharine Graham | Snowball |
| 1977 | Buffalo Evening News | $32.5M; 5-year antitrust battle | Snowball |
| 1987 | Salomon Inc. | 12% stake; became chairman during 1991 treasury scandal | Wikipedia, Berkshire letters |
| 1988 | Coca-Cola | Bought up to 7% for ~$1.02B; still holds | Berkshire letters |
| 1996 | GEICO investment accelerated | Buffett had been accumulating since 1976 | Berkshire letters |
| 1998 | General Re (Gen Re) | Acquired; later found "underwriting standards inadequate"; years of derivatives cleanup | Berkshire letters |
| 2002 | Currency forward contracts | $11B position; $2B+ gain by 2006 | Berkshire letters |
| 2008 | Became world's richest person | $62B net worth; NY Times op-ed: "Buy American. I am." | Forbes, NYT |
| 2009 | BNSF Railway | $34B ($44B including debt); largest acquisition to date | Berkshire letters |
| 2016 | Apple — began accumulating | Became largest holding by 2018 | Berkshire letters |
| 2020 | Exited airlines (Delta, American, Southwest, United) | Sold during COVID-19 crash | Berkshire 2020 letter |
| 2025 (May 3) | Greg Abel named successor CEO | Buffett remains chairman | Press reports |
